Chocolate Chip Cookies

My grandma's chocolate chip cookies were born out of love for her family. As a young mother during the holidays, she wanted to bake something special for her children. She used what she had on hand: a bag of chocolate pieces her neighbor had gifted her and her trusted pantry staples. Grandma would always say she didn't follow a recipe—she followed her heart. I remember sitting at the kitchen table as she explained how each cookie was made with an extra pinch of sugar "to sweeten the day." The smell of warm cookies filling her tiny kitchen remains a cherished memory.

Ingridients

  • 1 cup (226g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 1 cup (220g) packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 cups (375g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 cups (340g) chocolate chips

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, cream butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until fluffy.
  3. Add eggs one at a time, then stir in vanilla.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually blend into the wet ingredients.
  5. Fold in chocolate chips.
  6. Drop dough by rounded tablespoon onto prepared sheets.
  7. Bake 8-10 minutes, until edges are golden. Let cool on wire racks.

Banana Bread Muffins

The banana bread muffins were born out of necessity. Grandma hated wasting food, and when bananas ripened too quickly, she found a way to turn them into something magical. I remember mornings spent mashing bananas in a big bowl, Grandma chuckling as I made a mess. She'd tell me, “Every muffin has a story of something saved,” reminding me of the value in finding beauty in imperfection. Her muffins became a staple in our home, shared with neighbors, and always paired with her warm smile.

Ingridients

  • 1.5 cups (190g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 0.5 tsp baking powder
  • 0.5 tsp salt
  • 0.5 cup (113g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 0.75 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 0.25 cup (60ml) milk or buttermilk
  • Optional: 0.5 cup (85g) chocolate chips or chopped walnuts

Method

  1. Preheat the Oven:Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners or grease it lightly.
  2. Mix Dry Ingredients:In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  3. Prepare Wet Ingredients:In a large bowl, combine melted butter and sugar. Mix until smooth. Beat in the eggs and vanilla extract.
  4. Add Bananas: Stir in the mashed bananas and milk until well blended.
  5. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring until just combined. Do not overmix. Fold in chocolate chips or walnuts if using.
  6. Fill Muffin Tin: Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups, filling each about 2/3 full.
  7. Bake: Bake for 18-22 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Check at 18 minutes to avoid overbaking.
  8. Cool and Serve:Allow muffins to cool in the tin for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Classic Apple Pie

Apple pie wasn't just a recipe; it was a story in itself. Grandma learned the recipe from her own mother, who insisted that apples should always be picked fresh from the orchard. Every fall, we'd take a trip to the local farm, filling baskets with apples and laughing as we tasted the juiciest ones right off the tree. Back in her kitchen, Grandma would hum old songs as she rolled out the pie crust, teaching me to crimp the edges just so. She always said, “The secret ingredient isn't the cinnamon—it's our time together.” To this day, the smell of apple pie baking takes me back to those crisp autumn days.

Ingridients

Ingredients (Filling):

  • 6-7 medium apples, peeled, cored, and sliced (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)
  • 0.75 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 0.25 tsp nutmeg
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter

Ingredients (Crust):

  • 2.5 cups (310g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup (226g) cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 0.5 cup (120ml) ice water

Method

  1. Crust: In a bowl, mix flour and salt. Cut in butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add ice water, mixing until dough forms. Divide into two disks, wrap, and chill for 1 hour.
  2. Filling: Toss apples with sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice.
  3. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out one dough disk and line a pie dish. Add apple filling and dot with butter.
  4. Roll out the second disk, place it over the filling, and seal the edges. Cut slits on top for ventilation.
  5. Bake 50-60 minutes, until crust is golden and filling bubbles. Cool before serving.

Vanilla Cake

Grandma created her vanilla cake recipe for a very special occasion—her wedding anniversary with Grandpa. It was their 25th, and she wanted to make something simple yet meaningful. She spent days perfecting the balance of sweetness and richness. I still remember her beaming as Grandpa took his first bite and declared it “the best thing since the day I met you.” Later, she taught me how to make it, and we'd bake it together for birthdays, holidays, or just because. “Vanilla isn't plain,” she'd say. “It's the foundation of joy.”

Ingridients

Ingredients (Cake):

  • 2.5 cups (315g) all-purpose flour
  • 2.5 tsp baking powder
  • 0.5 tsp salt
  • 0.75 cup (170g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1.75 cups (350g) granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (240ml) whole milk

Ingredients (Buttercream Frosting):

  • 1 cup (226g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups (480g) powdered sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2-3 tbsp milk

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 8-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Cream butter and sugar until fluffy. Add eggs one at a time, then vanilla.
  4. Gradually add dry ingredients, alternating with milk, starting and ending with dry ingredients.
  5. Divide batter between pans and bake for 30-35 minutes, until a toothpick comes out clean. Cool before frosting.
  6. Frosting: Beat butter until creamy. Gradually add powdered sugar, vanilla, and milk until smooth. Frost the cooled cakes.
